Latitude vs. Max Temperature

This graph shows the relationship between geographic location and the maximum temperature that day in Farenheit on January 5th 2017. There are a few conclusions one can draw from the presented information.

The first thing that you can see is that the maximum temperature is centered near a latitude of 0 which makes sense considering that is the location of the equator and is often the warmest part of the planet on a day by day basis.

The next thing one can read from this graph is that as the latidue increases the maximum temperature decreases or to put it another way. The further north one travels the lower the temperature was on January 5th 2017 which should be largely unsurprising.

Finally if the latitude decreases or one moves to the south there is a far smaller impact on the maximum temperature being lowered this is due to the axial rotation of the planet pointing the southern hemisphere towards to sun from the September equinox to the March equinox. Or put simply winter in the northern hemisphere is summer in the southern hemisphere.
